About

Hi! I’m Myra, a first-generation college student graduating from Stony Brook University in Spring 2025.

I am creating FirstGen Forward (starting with this website) to help other first-gen students discover resources that can help them gain professional experience. Some of these tools I have personally used, and others I learned about through LinkedIn or by talking to people who found them helpful.

Everything I am sharing is based on my own experience and the resources I wish I had known about earlier in my career journey. For context, I come from a STEM background.

Important note: I am not a career coach or an advisor. I am simply sharing what has helped me and what I have learned along the way. Please take what resonates with you and always do your own research.

I hope you find something helpful here. Feel free to reach out through the contact form at the bottom of this page. I would love to hear from you!

Conferences

Why Attend Conferences?

Attending conferences, whether research-based or career-oriented, is a great way to connect with professionals, learn new skills, and explore career paths. Many conferences include career fairs, panels, and workshops to help students grow and succeed.

My Top Conference Tips

  • Bring a small notebook to take notes.
  • When networking, ask for their LinkedIn or another way to stay in touch.
  • Follow up with people a few days after the conference. Personalize your message by mentioning what you discussed.

Do Not Let Cost Stop You

Many conferences offer scholarships, free housing, travel reimbursement, or meal stipends. You can also talk to your career center or academic advisors about funding options available through your school.

Examples of Funded Conferences

Here are a few examples of conferences that offer scholarships, travel funding, or free housing for students:

CUWiP (Conference for Undergraduate Women in Physics)

A professional physics conference for undergraduate women and gender minorities. Many locations offer free housing, waived registration fees, travel funding, and full meal coverage.

IRTS Multicultural Career Workshop

A career conference for students interested in media, marketing, and communications. Many attendees can secure travel funding by working with their career centers.

Emerging Researchers National (ERN) Conference

Designed for underrepresented STEM students, this research conference provides fully funded airfare, hotel accommodations, and meals for participants.

More Conferences to Explore

  • NSBE National Conference: 400 plus career fair exhibitors, competitions, networking opportunities
  • Grace Hopper Celebration: 600 plus speakers, large career fairs, sessions for women in tech
  • Tapia Conference: Focus on diversity in computing, hackathons, and sessions on artificial intelligence

Many professional conferences do not require an application and simply involve registration. Scholarships for conference expenses may still be available through your school or through organizations that support first generation students. Be sure to ask your career center and academic advisors about how to fund your conference experience.
